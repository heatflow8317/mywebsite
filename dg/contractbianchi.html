Contracted Bianchi Identity: the statement

\[g^{ij}\nabla_i R_{jp}= \frac{1}{2} \nabla_p R\]

Proof

By the second Bianchi identity, \[\nabla_p R_{ijkl}+\nabla_i R_{jpkl}+\nabla_j R_{pikl}=0\] we multiply both sides by the factor \(g^{ik}g^{jl}\) and always remember that we "contract to the third index", that is, \[ [\partial_i, \partial_j ] \partial k=R^l_{ijk}\partial_l;\] \[g^{lp}R_{ijpk}=R^l_{ijk}\] we obtain \[ \begin{align} g^{ik}g^{jl}\left(\nabla_p R_{ijkl}+\nabla_i R_{jpkl}+\nabla_j R_{pikl} \right) &=0 \\ g^{ik}\left(\nabla_p (g^{jl}R_{jilk})-\nabla_i (g^{jl}R_{jplk})-\nabla_j (g^{jl} R_{pilk}) \right)&=0\\ g^{ik} (\nabla_p R_{ik}-\nabla_i R_{pk}-\nabla_j (g^{jl} R_{pilk}))&=0\\ \nabla_pR-\nabla_iR^i_p - \nabla_j (g^{ik}g^{jl} R_{pilk})&=0\\ \nabla_pR - \nabla_i R^i_p - \nabla_j R^j_p&=0\\ \nabla_pR&= 2\nabla_iR^i_p\\ g^{ij}\nabla_i R_{jp}&=\frac{1}{2}\nabla_pR. \end{align} \]
