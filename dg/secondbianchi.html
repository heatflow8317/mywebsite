Second Bianchi Identity: the statement

\[\nabla_p R_{ijkl}+\nabla_i R_{jpkl}+\nabla_j R_{pikl}=0\]

Proof

In normal coordinates, we have \[ \nabla_mR_{ijkl}=\partial_m(\partial_i\Gamma^k_{jl}-\partial_j \Gamma^k_{il}) = \partial_m \partial_i \Gamma^k_{jl}-\partial_m \partial_j \Gamma^k_{il} \] and therefore the given LHS is equal to \[ \partial_m \partial_i \Gamma^k_{jl}-\partial_m \partial_j \Gamma^k_{il}+ \partial_i \partial_j \Gamma^k_{ml}-\partial_i \partial_m \Gamma^k_{jl}+ \partial_j \partial_m \Gamma^k_{il}-\partial_j \partial_i \Gamma^k_{ml}=0 \]
