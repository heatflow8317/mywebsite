Jacobi field

  • A Jacobi field for a smooth distance function \(r\) is a smooth vector field \(J\) that does not depend on \(r\). In other words, it satisfies \[\mathcal{L}_{\partial r}J=0.\] Defining the operator \(S: X \mapsto \nabla_X \partial r\), we can calculate that \[ \begin{align} \mathcal{L}_{\partial r} J &= [\partial r, J] \\ &= \nabla_{\partial r}J - \nabla_J\partial r\\ &=\nabla_{\partial r}J - S(J) \end{align} \] and hence the Jacobi equation becomes \[\nabla_{\partial r}J=S(J).\] Differentiating both sides by \(\nabla_{\partial r}\) we obtain \[\nabla_{\partial r}\nabla_{\partial r} J = \nabla_{\partial r} S(J)=\nabla_{\partial r}\nabla_J \partial r =\nabla_{\partial r}\nabla_J \partial r -\nabla_J\nabla_{\partial r} \partial r =R(\partial r, J)\partial r= -R(J, \partial_r)\partial_r=-R_{\partial r}J\] which is the expression that we can obtain via second variation of arc-length.
  • Remark: \(|\nabla r|=1\).

    Relation to geometry

  • Claim: \(\nabla_{\partial r}\partial r=0\).
  • Proof of claim: \(|\nabla r|=1\).
  • Claim: (Riccati equation) \(\nabla_{\partial r} S + S^2 = -R_{\partial r}\).
  • Proof of claim: For any vector field \(X\), we have \[ \begin{align} (\nabla_{\partial r}S)X &= \nabla_{\partial r}(S(X))-S(\nabla_{\partial r}X)\\ &= -R_{\partial r}X-S(\nabla_X \partial r) \\ &= -R_{\partial r}X-S^2(X) \end{align} \]
  • Claim: \(\mathcal{L}_{\partial r}g = 2 Hess r\).
  • Proof of claim: This is the definition of Hessian in the sense of Lie derivatives.
  • Claim: \((\nabla_{\partial r} Hess r)(X,Y) + Hess^2r(X,Y)=-R(X,\partial_r, \partial_r, Y)\).
  • Proof of claim: we calculate that \[ \begin{align} (\nabla_{\partial r} Hess r)(X,Y) &= \nabla_{\partial r}(Hess r(X,Y))-Hess r(\nabla_{\partial r}X, Y) - Hess r(X, \nabla_{\partial r}Y) \\ &= \nabla_{\partial r}(g(X, S(Y)))- g(\nabla_{\partial r}X, S(Y))-g(\nabla_{\partial r}Y, S(X))\\ &= g(X, \nabla_{\partial r}S(Y))- g(S(Y),S(X))\\ &= g(X, \nabla_{\partial r}\nabla_Y \partial r)-Hess^2r(X,Y) \\ &= R(X, \partial r, Y, \partial r)-Hess^2r(X,Y) \\ &= -R(X,\partial_r, \partial_r, Y)-Hess^2r(X,Y). \end{align} \]
  • Claim: \((\mathcal{L}_{\partial r} Hess r)(X,Y) - Hess^2r(X,Y)=-R(X,\partial_r, \partial_r, Y)\).
