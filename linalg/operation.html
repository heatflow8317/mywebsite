  • Row and Column operations

  • Suppose we have a sequence of row operations joining two matrices \(A\) and \(B\): $$\require{AMScd} \begin{CD} A @>{\rho_1}>> @>{\rho_2}>> @>{\ldots}>> @>{\rho_n}>> B \end{CD}$$ where we recall the three types of row operations:
    • Row swap: \(R_i \leftrightarrow R_j\).
    • Scaling: \(R_i \rightarrow kR_i\) for some \(k\neq 0\).
    • Row sum: \(R_i \rightarrow kR_j + R_i\) for some \(k\neq 0\) and row index \(j\).
  • Remark: as in 2023-24, MATH1030 students at CUHK are not required to write down "\(R_i\rightarrow\)" for scaling and row sum.

  • Claim:

  • For each \(\rho_\alpha\) above, we can find a unique matrix \(H_\alpha\) such that the matrix resultant of \(A\) by \(\rho_\alpha\) is equal to \(H_\alpha A\). In other words, we have: $$\require{AMScd} \begin{CD} A @>{\rho_1}>> H_1A @>{\rho_2}>> H_2H_1A @>{\ldots}>> @>{\rho_n}>> H_n\ldots H_2H_1A=B \end{CD}$$

  • Proof of claim:

  • Fix \(A\) and denote by \(A'\) the matrix resultant after we perform \(\rho_1=R_1\leftrightarrow R_2\). Then \(A'_{1j}=A_{2j}, A'_{2j}=A_{1j}\) for all \(j\) and the other entries of \(A, A'\) are the same.
  • Let \(H_1\) be \(I \xrightarrow{\rho_1} H_1\). Consider \[ \begin{align} [H_1A]_{1j} &= \sum^n_{k=1} [H_1]_{1k}A_{kj} \\ &= \sum^n_{k=1} I_{2k} A_{kj}\\ &= A_{2j} \end{align} \] and similarly \([H_1A]_{2j}=A_{1j}\) for all row index \(j\).
  • The remaining proof is similar in the sense that we make use of matrix multiplication: \([AB]_{ij}=\sum^k_{i=1}A_{ik}B_{kj}.\)
  • The same philosophy can be applied to column operations, namely for any column operation \(\kappa\), \(A\xrightarrow{\kappa} A'\) implies \(A'=KA\) where \(I\xrightarrow{\kappa}K\).
  • In this way we can interpret left multiplication by an invertible matrix as a finite sequence of row operations, and right multiplication by an invertible matrix as a finite sequence of column operations.
  • Further, if we allow "zero-scaling" when defining operations, the interpretation above extends to (left or right) multiplication by a singular matrix.
