Ricci soliton
  • Let \((M, g_0)\) be a Riemannian manifold. Suppose \(g(t)\) is a Ricci flow such that \(g(0)=g_0\) and there exists some scaling factor \(\sigma(t)\) with \(\sigma(0)=1\) and one-parameter family of diffeomorphism \(\varphi(t)\) on \(M\) such that \[g(t)=\sigma(t)\varphi(t)^*g_0.\]
  • Claim: The following equation holds: \[\partial_t g(t) = \dot{\sigma}\varphi(t)^*g_0+\sigma\varphi(t)^*\mathcal{L}_{X(t)}g_0\] where \(X(t)\) is a vector field such that \(X(t)=\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}\varphi(t)\). Further, if \(X(0)=\nabla^{g_0} f\), then we have \[R_{ij}+\nabla_i\nabla_j f + \frac{\epsilon}{2}g_{ij}=0\] at time \(0\), where \(\epsilon=\dot{\sigma}\) is a constant for all \(t\).
  • Proof of claim: we observe that for any \(t=t_0\), we have \[ \begin{align} \frac{\partial}{\partial t}\bigg|_{t=t_0} \varphi(t)^*g &= \lim_{s\rightarrow 0} \frac{\varphi(t_0+s)^*-\varphi(t_0)^*}{s}g_0 \\ &=\lim_{s\rightarrow 0} \frac{\varphi(t_0+s)^*(\varphi(t_0)^{-1})^*-id}{s}\varphi(t_0)^*g_0 \\ &= \lim_{s\rightarrow \infty} \frac{(\varphi(t_0)^{-1}\varphi(t_0+s))^*-id}{s}\varphi(t_0)^*g_0 \\ &= \mathcal{L}_{\frac{\partial}{\partial t}|_{t=t_0}\varphi(t_0)^{-1}\varphi(t)}\varphi(t_0)^*g_0\\ &= \mathcal{L}_{(\varphi(t_0)^{-1})_*\frac{\partial}{\partial t}|_{t=t_0}\varphi(t)}\varphi(t_0)^*g_0\\ &= \mathcal{L}_{\varphi(t_0)^*\frac{\partial}{\partial t}|_{t=t_0}\varphi(t)}\varphi(t_0)^*g_0\\ &= \varphi(t_0)^*\mathcal{L}_{\frac{\partial}{\partial t}|_{t=t_0}\varphi(t)}g_0\\ &= \varphi(t_0)^*\mathcal{L}_{X(t_0)}g_0\\ \end{align} \] Hence, by using product rule, we deduce that \[ \begin{align} \partial_t g(t) &= \dot{\sigma}\varphi(t)^*g_0 + \sigma \varphi(t)^* \mathcal{L}_{X(t)}g_0 \\ -2Ric(g_0)&= \epsilon g_0 + \mathcal{L}_{X(0)}g_0 \\ -2R_{ij} &= \epsilon g_{ij} + \nabla_i X_j + \nabla_j X_i \\ -2R_{ij} &= \epsilon g_{ij} + 2\nabla_i\nabla_j f. \\ \end{align} \]
  • Conversely, given a metric \(g_0\), if it satisfies the Ricci gradient soliton equation above, then we can actually construct a Ricci gradient soliton.
  • Claim: Define \(\tau(t)=\epsilon t+1, X(t)=\frac{1}{\tau (t)}\nabla^{g_0}f, \varphi(t): X(t)= \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d} t}\varphi(t), f(t)=\varphi(t)^*f, g(t)=\tau(t)\varphi(t)^*g_0\), then \(g(t)\) solves the Ricci flow equation.
  • Proof of claim: we first observe how to scale a connection: suppose \(\nabla^g\) is the Levi-Civita connection for \(g\) and \(\nabla^{kg}\) is the Levi-Civita connection for \(kg\) where \(k\) is a contant in space. By the definition of gradient, for all smooth function \(f\) and smooth vector field \(X\) we have \[kg(\nabla^{kg}f, X) =Xf\] and hence \[g(k\nabla^{kg}f, X) = Xf = g(\nabla^g f, X).\] This implies \[\nabla^{kg}=\frac{1}{k}\nabla^g.\] We calculate that \[ \begin{align} \partial_t g(t) &= \epsilon \varphi(t)^*g_0 + \tau(t)\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d} t} \varphi(t)^*g_0\\ &= \epsilon \varphi(t)^*g_0 + \tau(t)\varphi(t)^*\mathcal{L}_{X(t)}g_0\\ &= \frac{\epsilon}{\tau(t)}g(t) + \tau(t)\mathcal{L}_{\varphi(t)^*X(t)}\varphi(t)^*g_0 \\ &=\frac{\epsilon}{\tau(t)}g(t) + \tau(t) \mathcal{L}_{\frac{1}{\tau(t)}\varphi(t)^*\nabla^{g_0}f(0)} \frac{1}{\tau(t)}g(t)\\ &=\frac{\epsilon}{\tau(t)}g(t) + \mathcal{L}_{\frac{1}{\tau(t)}\nabla^{\varphi(t)^*g_0}f(t)} g(t) \\ &=\frac{\epsilon}{\tau(t)}g(t) + \mathcal{L}_{\nabla^{\frac{1}{\tau(t)}\varphi(t)^*g_0}f(t)} g(t) \\ &=\frac{\epsilon}{\tau(t)}g(t) + \mathcal{L}_{\nabla^{g(t)}f(t)} g(t). \\ \end{align} \] On the other hand, \[ \begin{align} -2Ric(g(t))&= -2\varphi(t)^*Ric(g_0) \\ &= \varphi^*(t) \partial_t g(t)\\ &= \varphi(t)^* (\epsilon g_0 + \mathcal{L}_{\nabla^{g_0}f(0)}g_0) \\ &= \frac{\epsilon}{\tau(t)}g(t)+ \varphi(t)^*\mathcal{L}_{\nabla^{g_0}f(0)}g_0 \\ &= \frac{\epsilon}{\tau(t)}g(t)+ \mathcal{L}_{\varphi(t)^*\nabla^{g_0}f(0)} \varphi(t)^*g_0 \\ &= \frac{\epsilon}{\tau(t)}g(t)+ \mathcal{L}_{\nabla^{\varphi(t)^*g_0}f(t)} \frac{1}{\tau(t)}g(t) \\ &= \frac{\epsilon}{\tau(t)}g(t)+ \mathcal{L}_{\frac{1}{\tau(t)}\nabla^{\varphi(t)^*g_0}f(t)} g(t) \\ &= \frac{\epsilon}{\tau(t)}g(t)+ \mathcal{L}_{\nabla^{\tau(t)\varphi(t)^*g_0}f(t)} g(t) \\ &= \frac{\epsilon}{\tau(t)}g(t)+ \mathcal{L}_{\nabla^{g(t)}f(t)} g(t) \\ &= \partial_t g(t). \end{align} \] Hence \(g(t)\) is a gradient soliton. In the above calculations, we have obtained \[-2Ric(g(t))=\frac{\epsilon}{\tau(t)}g(t)+\mathcal{L}_{\nabla^{g(t)}f(t)}g(t).\] Locally this can be written as \[R_{ij}+\nabla_i\nabla_j f + \frac{\epsilon}{2\tau} g_{ij}=0\] for all \(t\) such that \(\tau(t)>0\).
  • Claim: We have \[\frac{\partial f}{\partial t} (t) = \bigg|\nabla^{g(t)}f(t) \bigg|^2_{g(t)}.\]
  • Proof of claim: \[ \begin{align} \frac{\partial f}{\partial t}(x,t) &= \frac{\partial}{\partial t} f\circ \varphi(t)(x)\\ &= g_0(\nabla^{g_0}f(0), \frac{\partial \varphi}{\partial t} )(x)\\ &= g_0(\nabla^{g_0}f(0), \frac{1}{\tau}\nabla^{g_0}f(0) )(\varphi(t)(x))\\ &= \frac{1}{\tau} g_0(\nabla^{g_0}f(0), \nabla^{g_0}f(0) )(\varphi(t)(x)) \\ &= \frac{1}{\tau} \varphi(t)^* g_0 (\nabla^{g_0}f(0), \nabla^{g_0}f(0))(x)\\ &= \frac{1}{\tau^2}g(t)(\nabla^{\varphi(t)^*g_0}f(t),\nabla^{\varphi(t)^*g_0}f(t) )\\ &= \frac{1}{\tau^2}g(t)(\nabla^{\frac{1}{\tau}g(t)}f(t), \nabla^{\frac{1}{\tau}g(t)}f(t))\\ &= \frac{1}{\tau^2}g(t)(\tau\nabla^{g(t)}f(t), \tau\nabla^{g(t)}f(t))\\ &= g(t)(\nabla^{g(t)}f(t),\nabla^{g(t)}f(t) )\\ &= |\nabla^{g(t)}f(t)|^2_{g(t)} \end{align} \]
