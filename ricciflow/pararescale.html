Parabolic Rescaling: the statement
Suppose \((M, g_t)\) is a Ricci flow on \(M\times I\). Let \(t\in [t_0-r^2,t_0]\subset I\) and \(\tilde{g}(\tilde{t})\) be the Ricci flow such that the following holds:
(1): \(\tilde{t}\in [-1,0]\)
(2): \(\tilde{g}(\tilde{t})=g(t)\); and
(3): \( \partial_{\tilde{t}} \tilde{g}(\tilde{t}) = -2 Ric_{\tilde{g}(\tilde{t})}\).
Then we have \[\tilde{g}(\tilde{t})=\frac{1}{r^2}g(t_0 + r^2 \tilde{t}).\]

Proof:
For any \(t\in [t_0-r^2, t_0]\) there exists a unique \(\tilde{t}\in [-1,0]\) such that \[t=t_0+r^2\tilde{t}.\] To be precise, we interpolate between the conditions: (i) when \(\tilde{t}=-1, t=t_0-r^2\) and (ii) when \(\tilde{t}=0, t=t_0\). This gives a straight line on the \(t-\tilde{t}\) plane \[\frac{\tilde{t}}{t-t_0}=\frac{-1-0}{t_0-r^2-t_0}=\frac{1}{r^2}.\] Hence \( r^2 \tilde{t}=t-t_0\) and \(t=t_0+r^2\tilde{t}\).
We suppose \(\tilde{g}(\tilde{t})=Cg(t)\) for some \(C\) independent of \(t\) and try to figure out \(C\). \[ \begin{align} \partial_{\tilde{t}} \tilde{g}(\tilde{t})&= -2Ric(\tilde{g}(\tilde{t}))\\ &= -2Ric(Cg(\frac{1}{r^2}(t-t_0)))\\ &= -2Ric(g(t))\\ &= \partial_t g(t) \end{align} \] On the other hand, \[ \begin{align} \partial_{\tilde{t}}\tilde{g}(\tilde{t})&= \partial_{\tilde{t}} Cg(t_0+r^2\tilde{t})\\ &= Cr^2 \partial_t g(t) \end{align} \] This forces \(Cr^2=1\) and therefore \(C=\frac{1}{r^2}\), so \[\tilde{g}(\tilde{t})=\frac{1}{r^2}g(t_0 + r^2 \tilde{t}).\]
