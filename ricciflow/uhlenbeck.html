Uhlenbeck's trick: the statement
The evolution of curvature has the following ugly expression: \[\partial_t R_{ijkl}=\Delta R_{ikjl} + 2(B_{ijkl}-B_{ijlk}-B_{iljk}+B_{ikjl})-(R^p_iR_{pjkl}+R^p_j{ipkl}+R^p_kR_{ijpl}+R^p_lR_{ijkp}).\] While one can simplify it with the \(*\) notation and write \[(\partial_t-\Delta) Rm=Rm * Rm-Rc * Rm\] instead, it is actually doing nothing. Alternatively, let \((V,h)\) be a vector bundle and \(u_0:(V,h)\rightarrow (TM, g_0)\) be a bundle isometry. We claim that \(u(t): (V,h)\rightarrow (TM, g(t))\) given by \[ \begin{align} u(0)&=u_0;\\ \partial_t u^i_a &= R^i_lu^l_a \end{align} \] remains an isometry along the flow, and \[\partial_t R_{abcd}=\Delta R_{abcd} +2(B_{abcd}-B_{abdc}+B_{acbd}-B_{adbc}).\]

Proof

\(\partial_t (u(t)^*g(t))_{ab}=\partial_t (u^i_au^j_b g_{ij})= R^i_lu^l_au^j_bg_{ij}+u^i_au^j_b g_{ij} -2 u^i_au^j_bR_{ij}=R_{ab}+R_{ab}-2R_{ab}=0\), so \(u(t)^*g(t)=u(0)g(0)=h\) for all \(t\). This shows that \(u(t)\) is an isometry for all \(t\). Further, we have \[ \begin{align} \partial_i R_{abcd} &= \partial_t (u^i_au^j_bu^k_cu^l_d R_{ijkl})\\ &= \sum_{cyc\{a,b,c,d\}} R^i_mu^m_au^j_bu^k_cu^l_d R_{ijkl} + u^i_au^j_bu^k_cu^l_d \partial_t R_{ijkl}\\ &= \Delta R_{abcd} + 2(B_{abcd}-B_{abdc}+B_{acbd}-B_{adbc}) \end{align} \]
