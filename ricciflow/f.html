  • First variation of the \(\mathcal{F}\)-functional

  • Suppose \(M\) is a compact manifold. Let \(\delta g_{ij}=v_{ij}, \delta f=h\) be variations where \(g\) is a metric on \(M\) and \(f\) is a smooth function on \(M\). Then \(v_{ij}\) is a symmetric covariant 2-tensor on \(M\). Define the \(\mathcal{F}\)-functional to be \[\mathcal{F}(g,f)=\int_M(R+|\nabla f|^2)e^{-f}\mathrm{d} V,\] then the first variation of \(\mathcal{F}\) is a functional on \(v_{ij}, h\) given by \[\delta \mathcal{F}(v_{ij}, h)= \int_M e^{-f}\left[-v_{ij}(R_{ij}+\nabla_i\nabla_j f)+ \left( \frac{v}{2}-h\right)(2\Delta f - |\nabla f|^2 + R) \right]\mathrm{d}V.\]
  • Proof:
  • Claim 1: the first variation of Christoffel symbol is \[\delta \Gamma^k_{ij}=\frac{1}{2}g^{kl}(\nabla_i v_{jl}+\nabla_j v_{il} - \nabla_l v_{ij}).\] Proof of claim 1: We claim the equation holds at each point \(x\in M\) as functions. Fix \(x\). Choose normal coodinates at \(x\), then locally we have \[ \begin{align} \delta \Gamma^k_{ij} &= \delta \left( \frac{1}{2}g^{kl}(\partial_i g_{jl}+\partial_j g_{il} - \partial_l g_{ij}) \right)\\ &= \frac{1}{2}(\delta g^{kl}) (\partial_i g_{jl}+\partial_j g_{il} - \partial_l g_{ij}) + \frac{1}{2}g^{kl}(\partial_i v_{jl}+\partial_j v_{il} - \partial_l v_{ij}) \\ &= frac{1}{2}g^{kl}(\partial_i v_{jl}+\partial_j v_{il} - \partial_l v_{ij})\\ &= \frac{1}{2}g^{kl}(\nabla_i v_{jl}+\nabla_j v_{il} - \nabla_l v_{ij}) \end{align} \] where in the last step we change back to covariant derivatives.
  • Claim 2: the first variation of scalar curvature is (\(v=g^{ji}v_{ij}\)) \[\delta R = -\Delta v + \nabla_i\nabla_j v_{ij}-R_{ij}v_{ij}\] Proof of claim 2: Writing in covariant derivatives, we have \[\delta R_{ij}=\nabla_p (\delta \Gamma^p_{ij})-\nabla_i (\delta \Gamma^p_{pj}).\] \[ \begin{align} \nabla_p \delta \Gamma^p_{ij} &= \nabla_p \frac{1}{2}g^{pl}(\nabla_i v_{jl}+\nabla_j v_{il} - \nabla_l v_{ij})\\ &= \frac{1}{2}g^{pl}(\nabla_p\nabla_iv_{jl}+\nabla_p\nabla_jv_{il}-\nabla_p\nabla_lv_{ij}) \end{align} \] \[ \begin{align} \nabla_i \delta \Gamma^p_{pj} &= \nabla_i \frac{1}{2}g^{pl}(\nabla_p v_{jl}+\nabla_j v_{pl} - \nabla_l v_{pj})\\ &= \frac{1}{2}g^{pl}(\nabla_i\nabla_jv_{pl}) \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\nabla_i\nabla_j v \end{align} \] the two terms cancel out because, if we interchange \(p,l\) they are the same. Hence, we have \[ \begin{align} \delta R &= \delta (g^{ji}R_{ij}) \\ &= (\delta g^{ji}) R_{ij} + g^{ji} \delta R_{ij} \\ &= -g^{pj}g^{qi} \delta g_{pq} R_{ij} + g^{ji} \left[\frac{1}{2}g^{pl}(\nabla_p\nabla_iv_{jl}+\nabla_p\nabla_j v_{il} -\nabla_p\nabla_l v_{ij})-\frac{1}{2}\nabla_i\nabla_j v \right] \\ &= -g^{pj}g^{qi} v_{pq} R_{ij} + \frac{1}{2} \left[g^{ji}g^{pl}(2\nabla_p\nabla_iv_{jl}-\Delta v)-\Delta v \right] \\ &= -g^{pj}g^{qi} v_{pq} R_{ij} + g^{ji}g^{pl}(\nabla_p\nabla_iv_{jl}) -\Delta v\\ &= -v_{ij}R_{ij} + \nabla_i\nabla_j v_{ij} - \Delta v \end{align} \] where in the last step we used normal coordinates and then change back to covariant coordinates. The \(i,j\) in the last term are dummy indexes for summation.
  • Claim 3: The first variation of \(|\nabla f|^2\) is \(\delta |\nabla f|^2=-v_{ij}\nabla_i\nabla_jf+2\langle \nabla h, \nabla f\rangle\). The proof is omitted.
  • Claim 4: THe first variation of \(\mathrm{d}V\) is \(\delta \mathrm{d}V = \frac{v}{2}\mathrm{d}V\). The proof is omitted.
  • Putting all the above together, we obtain \[\delta \mathcal{F}=\int_M e^{-f} \left[-\Delta v + \nabla_i\nabla_jv_{ij}+v_{ij}R_{ij}-v_{ij}\nabla_if\nabla_jf +2\langle \nabla f, \nabla h\rangle + (R+|\nabla f|^2)(\frac{v}{2}-h) \right]\mathrm{d}V\] Using \(\Delta e^{-f}=(|\nabla f|^2-\Delta f)e^{-f}\) and integration by parts, we can reduce the formula to the desired result. The main idea is to remove the derivatives of \(v,h\).
