DeTurck's trick

  • Consider the Ricci flow equation \[\partial_t g_{ij} = -2 R_{ij}\] By evolution of Ricci curvature, we have \[D[-2Rc](h)=g^{pq}(\nabla_p \nabla_q h_{ij} + \nabla_i\nabla_j h_{pq}-\nabla_q\nabla_i h_{jp}-\nabla_q \nabla_j h_{ip})\] and our goal is to find a parabolic system \[\partial_t \bar{g}_{ij}=P(\bar{g})\] and a one parameter family of diffeomorphisms \(\varphi_t\) such that \(g=\varphi^*_t \bar{g}\) solves the Ricci flow.
  • Observe that given \(g=\varphi^*_t \bar{g}\) we actually have \[\partial_t g = \partial_t \varphi^*_t \bar{g}=\varphi^*_t P(\bar{g}_t)+\varphi^*_t \mathcal{L}_{\partial_t \varphi_t}\bar{g}_t\]
  • Set up ODE: by ODE theory, solution \(\varphi_t\) exists once we found \(W\). \[ \begin{align} \partial_t \varphi_t &= W(t)\\ \varphi_0 &= id \end{align} \] then we have \[-2\varphi^*_tRic(\bar{g})=\varphi^*_t P(\bar{g}_t)+\varphi^*_t \mathcal{L}_W\bar{g}_t\] and hence \[-2Ric(\bar{g})=P(\bar{g}_t)+\mathcal{L}_W\bar{g}_t\] In other words, the Ricci curvature operator differs from a (strictly) parabolic operator by a Lie derivative.
  • Below, we work on \(\bar{g}\) but omit the bar due to laziness. Calculate: \[ \begin{align} &\hspace{0.7cm} g^{pq}(\nabla_p \nabla_q h_{ij} + \nabla_i\nabla_j h_{pq}-\nabla_q\nabla_i h_{jp}-\nabla_q\nabla_j h_{ip})\\ &= g^{pq}\bigg(\nabla_p \nabla_q h_{ij} + \frac{1}{2}\nabla_i\nabla_j h_{pq}-\nabla_i\nabla_q h_{jp}\\ &\hspace{3.6cm} + \frac{1}{2}\nabla_j\nabla_i h_{pq}-\nabla_j\nabla_q h_{ip}\bigg) + L.O.T.\\ &= g^{pq} \bigg( \nabla_p\nabla_qh_{ij} + \nabla_i \bigg( \frac{1}{2}\nabla_j h_{pq}-\nabla_q h_{jp}\bigg) \\ &\hspace{3.7cm} +\nabla_j \bigg( \frac{1}{2}\nabla_i h_{pq}-\nabla_q h_{ip}\bigg) \bigg) + L.O.T. \\ &= g^{pq}\nabla_p\nabla_qh_{ij}\\ &\hspace{1cm} - g^{pq}\nabla_i \bigg( \frac{1}{2}\bigg) \bigg(\nabla_q h_{jp}+\nabla_ph_{jq}-\nabla_j h_{pq}\bigg) \\ &\hspace{1cm} - g^{pq}\nabla_j \bigg( \frac{1}{2}\bigg) \bigg(\nabla_q h_{ip}+\nabla_ph_{iq}-\nabla_i h_{pq}\bigg) + L.O.T. \\ &= g^{pq}\nabla_p\nabla_qh_{ij} -\nabla_i \Gamma^l_{pq}(h)g_{lj}g^{pq}-\nabla_j \Gamma^l_{pq}(h)g_{li}g^{pq} +L.O.T.\\ &= g^{pq}\nabla_p\nabla_qh_{ij} + D[\nabla_i W_j - \nabla_j W_i](h)+L.O.T.\\ &= D[P](h)+ D[\nabla_i W_j - \nabla_j W_i](h)+L.O.T. \end{align} \] if we define \(W_i=-g^{pq}g_{ij}(\Gamma^j_{pq}-\tilde{\Gamma}^j_{pq})\) and \(P\) be Laplacian.
  • The tilde term serves for making \(W\) tensorial and only contributes to the lower order terms.
  • Now we have \(\bar{g}\), which gives \(W\), followed by \(\varphi_t\), finally \(g\) for the Ricci flow.
  • Short time existence of RF follows from standard parabolic theory.
