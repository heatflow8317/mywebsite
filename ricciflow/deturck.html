DeTurck's Trick: the statement
Given any Ricci flow \((M, g)\), the induced Ricci-Deturck flow (as an equation in \(\bar{g}\)) \[\partial_t \bar{g}_{ij} = -2Ric(\bar{g})_{ij}+\nabla_iW_j+\nabla_jW_i\] is strictly parabolic, where \(W^k=g^{pq}(\Gamma^k_{pq}-\tilde{\Gamma}^k_{pq})\) for some fixed background metric \(\tilde{g}\) and the Chiristoffel symbol \(\tilde{\Gamma}\) is determined by \(\nabla^{LC}(\tilde{g})\).
Moreover, this implies that the Ricci Flow equation always have short time existence.
