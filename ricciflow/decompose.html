Decomposition of curvature: the statement
Let \(Rm\) works like a section of \(\mathcal{E}=Sym^2(\wedge^2 T^*M)\) as follows: for any \(\phi, \psi\in \wedge^2 T^*M\), define \[Rm(\phi, \psi)=R_{ijlk}\phi^{ij}\psi^{kl}.\] Uhlenbeck's trick tells us \[(*): \partial_t R_{abcd}=2(B_{abcd}-B_{abdc}+B_{acbd}-B_{adbc})\] where \( B_{abcd}=-R^e_{fab}R^f_{ecd} \). Suppose now \(M\) is of dimension 3, then we actually have the basis \[\{e_1\wedge e_2, e_2\wedge e_3, e_3 \wedge e_1\}\] for \(\wedge^2T^*M\), where \(\{e_1, e_2, e_3\}\) is an orthonormal basis of \(TM\).
Diagoanlize \(Rm\) in \(\mathcal{E}\) with the basis \(\{e_1\wedge e_2, e_2\wedge e_3, e_3 \wedge e_1\}\), (*) then becomes \[\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d} t}\left(\begin{array}{ccc}\lambda_1&0&0\\0&\lambda_2&0\\0&0&\lambda_3 \end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ccc}\lambda_1^2+\lambda_2\lambda_3&0&0\\0&\lambda_2^2+\lambda_3\lambda_1&0 \\0&0&\lambda_3^2+\lambda_1\lambda_2\end{array} \right).\]

Ricci and scalar curvature

We claim that:

In the above setting, we have \[Rc=\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin{array}{ccc}\lambda_2+\lambda_3&0&0\\0&\lambda_3+\lambda_1&0 \\0&0&\lambda_1+\lambda_2\end{array} \right)\] and \[R=\lambda_1+\lambda_2+\lambda_3.\]

Proof of claim:

Recall that \(R_{ij}=\sum_pR_{ippj}\), so \(R_{11}=R_{1221}+R_{1331}\). Under this algebraic setting, namely we consider the space of 2-vectors instead of the usual tangent vectors, we modify our definition of the sectional curvature to be \[K(e_1,e_2)=\frac{R_{1221}}{||e_1\wedge e_2||^2}.\] Since \(||e_1\wedge e_2||^2=||e_1\otimes e_2-e_2\otimes e_1||^2=2\), we actually have \[K(e_1,e_2)=2R_{1221}\] where we did defined \(K(e_1,e_2)=R_{1221}\) before. Hence, \[R_{11}=\frac{1}{2}(K(e_1\wedge e_2)+K(e_1, e_3))=\frac{1}{2}(\lambda_3+\lambda_2).\]

Kulkarni-Nomizu product, Einstein tensor and Weyl tensor.

We define the Einstein tensor to be the traceless part of the Ricci tensor: \[E=Rc^\circ=Rc-\frac{1}{n}Rg_{ij}.\] We define the Kulkarni-Nomizu product of symmetric tensors as follows: \[\alpha \bigcirc\mspace{-10.5pt}\wedge\mspace{3pt} \beta= \alpha_{ik}\beta_{jl}+\alpha_{jl}\beta_{ik}-\alpha_{jk}\beta_{il}-\alpha_{il}\beta_{jk}.\] We define the Weyl tensor \(W\) to be: \[W=Rm-\frac{1}{n-2}Rc^\circ \bigcirc\mspace{-10.5pt}\wedge\mspace{3pt} g - \frac{R}{2n(n-1)}g\bigcirc\mspace{-10.5pt}\wedge\mspace{3pt} g= Rm-\frac{1}{n-2}Rc \bigcirc\mspace{-10.5pt}\wedge\mspace{3pt} g + \frac{R}{2(n-1)(n-2)}g\bigcirc\mspace{-10.5pt}\wedge\mspace{3pt} g.\]
