Einstein metric

Let \((M, g)\) be Einstein. If \(dim M\geq 3\) then \((M,g)\) has constant scalar curvature. Further, if \(dim M=3\), then \((M,g)\) has constant sectional curvature.

Proof

By the contracted Bianchi identity, \[g^{ij}\nabla_i R_{jp}= \frac{1}{2} \nabla_p R\] Put \(E_{jp}=R_{jp}-\frac{1}{n}Rg_{jp}\) we obtain \[g^{ij}\nabla_i E_{jp}=g^{ij}\nabla_i\left( R_{jp}-\frac{1}{n}Rg_{jp}\right) =\frac{1}{2}\nabla_pR-\frac{1}{n}\nabla_i \delta^i_p R=\frac{1}{2}\nabla_pR-\frac{1}{n} \nabla_pR=\left(\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{n}\right)\nabla_pR.\] For \(n\geq 3\), the LHS is 0 if and only if \(R=0\).
Besides, when \(n=3\), we can find a basis such that \(Rc\) is diagonal and \(E=0\) gives \(Rc=\frac{R}{3}g\). \[Rc=\frac{1}{2} \left(\begin{array}{ccc} \lambda_2+\lambda_3&0&0\\ 0&\lambda_3+\lambda_1&0\\ 0&0&\lambda_1+\lambda_2 \end{array}\right) =\frac{R}{3}g\\ = \left(\begin{array}{ccc} \frac{R}{3}&0&0\\ 0&\frac{R}{3}&0\\ 0&0&\frac{R}{3} \end{array}\right) \] this forces \(\lambda_1=\lambda_2=\lambda_3=R\) and hence constant sectional curvature.
